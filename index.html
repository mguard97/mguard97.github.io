Portfolio

Steeelth: Senior Game Design Capstone

Avoid enemies and explore in this stealth-puzzle oriented action-adventure experience. This project was made for my senior Game Design capstone at OSU. The class required weekly builds and public presentations, culminating in a semester showcase.

  • AI

    I created a Unity implementation of Goal Oriented Action Planning (GOAP) originally developed by Jeff Orkin for F.E.A.R. (2005) (link) The GOAP backbone drove virtually all dynamic AI behaviors, from both hostile and non-combatative, to security cameras and searchlights. Additionally, my implementation allows each instance's priorities to be restructured or extended at runtime. In the final game, this was used to give units roles such as guard (which would defend a location rather than seek out the player) or hunter (which does the opposite), but I have plans to extend this into a larger investigation system.

  • Tool Design

    To better enable the other programmers on our team, I created a suite of Unity Editor extensions to facilitate content development, behavior design, level layouts and level annotations. As a result of the productivity boost offered by these tools, our team was able to reach stretch goals we couldn't otherwise.

  • Project Management

    I was chiefly responsible for build stability, project continuity and accountability. I drove the creation of new tasks and empowered my team members to excel in their own work, offerring assistance at their convenience when learning new features of Unity, or approaching challenging technical problems. I collaborated on regular documentation including development video logs and public presentations.

Anything Grows!: ACCAD Senior Capstone (Active Development)

Roleplay as a crash-landed astronaut learning how to farm to survive in space. In medium-length experience, combine items in unexpected ways to create plants with useful features -- such as a coke geyser to gain altitude or a fire plant to destroy barriers-- and trade your way to escape via spaceship.

  • Systems Design

    I created a clockwork system which dispatched events based on a game-relative time of day. Based on this system, I implemented a farming system which tracked the growth of plants consistently over the course of multiple saves and even while the level was unloaded with minimal performance overhead. These systems in combination enabled other programmers to create features based on daily schedules and particular calendar dates.

    I also implemented a data-oriented implementation of common game objects lie plants and inventory items, which enabled easy content development for designers of diffent technical ability. Additionally, I was principally responsible for the flow of gameplay, saving and loading, and delivering builds for distributed testing.

Design Centric Maze Generation

As an undergraduate research assistant, I collaborated on the development of a level design tool in Unity implementing a novel maze generation algorithm. Designers input constraints such as how many turns are desired or the length of the longest path, and the tool delivers multiple layouts (at reasonable sizes) near instantaneously. A version of the tool was presented at the PCG Workshop 2019 in San Francisco, CA.

The ACM Digital listing of the paper is available here:

Kim, Paul Hyunjin, Jacob Grove, Skylar Wurster, Roger Crawfis, Design-Centric Maze Generation, Proceedings of the PCG Workshop 2019. Procedural Content Generation, San Luis Obispo, California USA. https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3337722.3341854

The slide presentation from the PCG workshop is available here.
